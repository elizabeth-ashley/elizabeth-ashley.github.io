Background

As an advocate for science communication, I enjoy bridging the gap between science and design — combining my technical knowledge with my keen eye for design to create a beautiful science. My goal is to always create easily understood visual science that is informative and engaging with pixel-perfect user experiences. I want to empower young people from all walks of life to pursue STEM, explore the outdoors and recognize how art can be a useful tool for communicating science.

I'm currently a Raster Product Engineer with the Imagery & Remote Sensing team. I focus and work on UX/UI design and development of RADAR & SAR based capabilities at Esri. I have a B.S. in Geophysics from the University of Texas, Austin and a Masters degree in Geophysics from the University of Colorado, Boulder where I studied remote sensing applications to induced seismicity. I have worked for the Department of the Interior U.S. Bureau of Reclamation as a Seismic Hazards Intern and for Denali National Park & Preserve, as a Science Communicator. I have also worked for Halliburton Energy Services Inc., as a Field Engineer, and as a Geosteering Geoscientist.

As a Graduate Student, I spend my time studying the impact human activities such as wastewater injection wells, geothermal energy production, mining and explosions have on inducing earthquakes. I use seismic data and satellite imagery to provide evidence of these impacts.

When I'm not in front of a computer screen, I'm probably studying rocks, climbing rocks, and painting rocks!
