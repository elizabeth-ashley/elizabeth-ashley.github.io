Background

I'm currently a Graduate Student at the University of Colorado, Boulder studying Geophysics. In 2018 I graduated from University of Texas. Since then I have worked at Denali National Park & Preseve Murie Science and Learning Center, as a Science Communicator Halliburton Energy Services Inc., as a Field Engineer and as a Geosteering Geoscientist.

As a geophysicist, I enjoy bridging the gap between engineering and design — combining my technical knowledge with my keen eye for design to create a beautiful product. My goal is to always build applications that are scalable and efficient under the hood while providing engaging, pixel-perfect user experiences. I am a PhD student at the University of Colorado Boulder. I spend my time here studying the impact human activities such as wastewater injection wells, geothermal energy production, mining and explosions have on inducing earthquakes. As a geophysicist I am trying to reduce these earthquake occurrences using seismic data and satellite imagery. I want to empower young people from all walks of life to pursue STEM, explore the outdoors and recognize how art can be a useful tool for communicating science. That's why I am so psyched about science commmunication.

When I'm not in front of a computer screen, I'm probably studying rocks, climbing rocks, and painting rocks!
